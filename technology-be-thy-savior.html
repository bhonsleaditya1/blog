Technology be thy Savior

Often I come across techies complaining about improper use of methodologies and technology. It was hard to believe large tech corporations facing this issue like these. How is something so basic not observed? Experiencing them in person, I realized that it's not only accurate but deeply engraved in tech.

Technology becomes a burden

Technology has and is advancing at such a pace that keeping pace with it is often difficult. What is even more difficult is to unlearn and adopt new methodologies. Often we find ourselves following it incorrectly, usually the anti-pattern of the same. In programming, you need to know what you are doing, as Allen Holub says "Enough rope to shoot yourself in the foot".

SOLID Principles

Most basic principles solve many issues & reduce technical debt, but often overlooked for delivery. Stressed when one learns to program but hardly applied.

Software Design Principles

Ignoring this is so rampant and its knowledge so uncommon sometimes I feel I have otherworldly knowledge. Applying this correctly is on another level. Getting this right often requires practice.

Struggling with Monolith in the Age of Microservices

Not that Monolith is wrong for all purposes. Areas that are proven and tested where microservices will outperform. Teams struggling with conversion to microservices are often unwilling to move from it.

True Agile Development

Agile is dead, long live (Anti-)Agile. Most of us get introduced to anti-agile, so discovering and following agile is a feat. There are so many rants on this that I can relate to everyone of them. If anyone of you is working in an agile environment you're lucky.

Lack of Engineering

Many times coding feels mundane. I have felt it firsthand and heard this from friends and colleagues since my college days. There is nothing new to explore or solve or create impact.
